Description:
Abstract:
This layer represents the Mid-Region Council of Governments (MRCOG) 4.5 county area - Bernalillo, Sandoval, Torrance, and Valencia Counties and southern Santa Fe County in central New Mexico. Counties are divided into data analysis sub-zones (DASZs). DASZs are contiguous and cover the entire county area. Attribute information includes 2016 estimates and 2040 projections for population, housing, and employment.

The 2016 population estimates were derived from Esri Business Analyst Online. Esri uses a cohort survival model to carry the 2010 Census population block data forward to 2016. The 2016 housing figures were derived by advancing the 2010 Census block data to 2016 using residential building permits issued between 2010 and 2015. The 2016 employment estimate is based employment data purchased from a third party vendor, Infogroup. This data was checked and verified through phone calls, internet searches and against previous employment databases. This file was smoothed to match county control targets that were independently derived from the Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages by County supplemented with uncovered military, self-employment, and agricultural employment estimates.

The regional projection for population is based on county-level population projections developed by the University of New Mexico’s Geospatial and Population Studies (GPS) in 2016. The regional employment projection was developed by MRCOG using a combination of sources including University of New Mexico’s Bureau of Business and Economic Research (BBER) and a locally customized Regional Economic Model (REMI). The DASZ level forecast is developed with the assistance of a land use simulation modeling system (UrbanSim) that is estimated and calibrated specifically for the MRCOG region. Inputs include existing population (US Census Bureau and Esri), employment (Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages and InfoGroup), land use inventories, recent building permits, development review cases, local plans (including sector plans, area plans, master plans, etc.), local zoning regulation (including allowable uses and densities), vacancy rates, development costs, building square footage, undevelopable lands, and information collected through developer and planner interviews.
Purpose:
Mid-Region Council of Governments (MRCOG) DASZ datasets describe current and forecast socioeconomic characteristics, including population, housing, and employment. DASZs (data analysis subzones) represent transportation planning zones, which are small units of geography compatible with MRCOG's transportation model. These datasets are created by MRCOG to understand existing transportation trends and project future travel demand in the region. Their use extends beyond travel demand forecasting to land use planning and economic development.

Data Quality Information:

Attribute Accuracy:
Attribute Accuracy Report:
Values in the DASZ2010 field were reviewed for frequency and were found to be unique (i.e., each value occurs only once). Values for population, housing, and employment should be considered estimates for 2016 and projections for 2040. All records have a value in every field. Some values may be zero (0), but there are no null (NoData, NaN, etc.) values.
Logical Consistency Report:
DASZs are contiguous and cover the entire MRCOG region and entire county areas (except for Santa Fe County). Geometry and attributes were evaluated with ArcGIS geoprocessing tools, such as Check Geometry, and Python scripts using ArcPy and other modules.
Completeness Report:
Any gap in regional coverage should be considered an error in geometry and not an intentional omission. Santa Fe County is the exception; the MRCOG region only includes southern Santa Fe County. All records have a value in every field. Some values may be zero (0), but there are no null (NoData, NaN, etc.) values.

Spatial Data Organization Information:

Indirect Spatial Reference Method:

DASZs represent transportation planning zones, which are small units of geography compatible with MRCOG's transportation model. They are generally smaller than census tracts and in some cases may nest within tracts. Their boundaries are generally visible features of the landscape. In the attribute table, each DASZ has a unique ID and a record of the county in which it is located.
